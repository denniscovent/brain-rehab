## 1. Executive Summary

**Unrot Your Brain** is a mobile-first web application designed to help users rehabilitate their attention spans and cognitive abilities after prolonged exposure to digital overstimulation. The app provides a structured 7-day challenge with daily tasks, journaling prompts, progress tracking, and curated brain exercise ideas.

The product addresses a growing concern among knowledge workers and former high-achievers who feel their mental acuity has diminished due to excessive social media use, infinite scrolling, and constant digital stimulation.

### 1.1 Problem Statement

Many individuals who once identified as intellectually curious and sharp have experienced cognitive decline characterized by:

- Inability to focus on long-form content
- Shortened attention spans
- Difficulty generating original thoughts
- Loss of curiosity and passion for learning
- Dependence on external stimulation

### 1.2 Solution

A progressive 7-day rehabilitation program delivered through an installable web app that guides users through evidence-based exercises to reclaim their cognitive abilities, including:

- Digital detox practices
- Intentional boredom
- Focused reading
- Hands-on activities
- Reflective journaling

## 2. Inspiration & Context

### 2.1 Source Material

This product is inspired by the essay *"Unrot Your Brain"* which articulates the experience of cognitive atrophy in the digital age. The essay resonates with individuals who once saw themselves as "the smart one" but now struggle with basic attention and focus.

**Key quote from source:**
> "There was a time I devoured books like candy... My favorite characters were Hermione and Annabeth Chase — the smart girls. I saw myself in them, and I liked that."

### 2.2 Key Insights from Source

1. **Identity Loss:** Users don't just lose focus—they lose a core part of their identity tied to intellectual capability
2. **Gradual Decline:** The transition from "rest" to "rot" happens imperceptibly
3. **Rehabilitation Framing:** The solution is framed as physical therapy for the mind—gentle, progressive, patient
4. **Intrinsic Motivation:** Activities should be done for their own sake, not productivity or external validation
5. **Tactile & Analog:** Emphasis on physical books, handwriting, and hands-on activities

### 2.3 Target User Profile

**Primary:** Adults aged 22-40 who were once high-achieving students or voracious readers, now experiencing attention difficulties. They likely:
- Work in knowledge work
- Remember a time before smartphones dominated
- Feel genuine distress about their diminished cognitive abilities

**Secondary:** Anyone feeling overwhelmed by digital consumption who wants a structured approach to reclaiming their attention and curiosity.

## 3. Product Overview

### 3.1 Core Value Proposition

A judgment-free, structured program that treats cognitive rehabilitation like physical therapy—gentle progression, daily exercises, and tracking to help users rediscover their intellectual identity.

### 3.2 Key Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **7-Day Program** | Themed daily challenges progressing from digital detox to cognitive reclamation |
| **Daily Tasks** | 5 specific, time-boxed activities per day with clear instructions |
| **Progress Tracking** | Visual progress ring, streak counter, and weekly overview |
| **Journaling** | Daily prompts with saved entries to track personal growth |
| **Ideas Library** | Curated brain exercises, reading challenges, and hobby suggestions |
| **Offline Support** | Full functionality without internet connection |
| **Installable PWA** | Add to home screen for native app experience |

### 3.3 Design Philosophy

- Dark, calming interface to reduce visual stimulation
- Motivational quotes from source material to maintain emotional connection
- Minimal gamification—progress tracking without addiction mechanics
- Mobile-first but desktop-compatible
- No account required—privacy-respecting local storage

## 4. 7-Day Program Structure

### Day 1: Digital Detox Begins
**Theme:** Breaking the scroll cycle and introducing intentional phone-free time.

**Quote:** *"Step away from the infinite scroll. Let your brain remember how to think without being spoon-fed."*

| Task | Duration |
|------|----------|
| 3-Hour Phone Fast (put phone in another room) | 3 hrs |
| 10 Minutes of Boredom (no stimulation, just exist) | 10 min |
| Brain Dump Writing (freewrite) | 10 min |
| Delete One Social Media App | 2 min |
| Evening Reflection (3 sentences about attention) | 5 min |

**Journal Prompt:** What made you reach for your phone today? How did it feel to resist?

---

### Day 2: Embrace the Stillness
**Theme:** Learning to sit with discomfort and silence.

**Quote:** *"You don't need to be entertained every second. Let yourself sit in silence."*

| Task | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Morning Without Phone (first hour) | 1 hr |
| 15-Minute Boredom Session (Navy SEAL nap technique) | 15 min |
| 15-Minute Freewrite | 15 min |
| 20-Minute Phone-Free Walk | 20 min |
| Read 10 Pages (physical book preferred) | 20 min |

**Journal Prompt:** What thoughts came up during your boredom sessions? Were any surprising?

---

### Day 3: Rediscover Curiosity
**Theme:** Choosing a fascination topic and beginning research.

**Quote:** *"Pick a topic. Become obsessed. Just for the thrill of it."*

| Task | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Choose Your Fascination Topic | 10 min |
| 30-Minute Research Session (handwritten notes) | 30 min |
| 4-Hour Total Phone Limit | All day |
| 15-Minute Boredom Practice | 15 min |
| Write About Your Learning | 10 min |

**Journal Prompt:** What topic did you choose? What drew you to it? What surprised you in your research?

---

### Day 4: Reading Resurrection
**Theme:** Rebuilding the reading habit with physical books.

**Quote:** *"Read like a kid again—greedy, curious, unselfconscious."*

| Task | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Library Trip or Book Hunt | 30 min |
| 30-Minute Uninterrupted Reading | 30 min |
| No Phone First 2 Hours of Day | 2 hrs |
| Continue Research on Fascination Topic | 20 min |
| Handwrite Something (letter, poem, summary) | 15 min |

**Journal Prompt:** What are you reading? How does it feel to hold a physical book again?

---

### Day 5: Hands & Focus
**Theme:** Engaging in tactile, hands-on activities.

**Quote:** *"Give your brain something real to touch. Something with friction. With error."*

| Task | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Start a Hands-On Project (cooking, crafting, building) | 30 min |
| Read 20+ Pages | 30 min |
| 45-Minute Deep Research Session | 45 min |
| Task Without Media (chores in silence) | 20 min |
| Evening Writing Reflection | 15 min |

**Journal Prompt:** What did you create or work on with your hands? How did focused manual work feel?

---

### Day 6: Mental Play
**Theme:** Engaging in intellectual challenges and debates.

**Quote:** *"Sit with a question, really sit with it, without reaching for a distraction."*

| Task | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Big Question Session (5 unanswered questions) | 20 min |
| Debate Yourself (argue both sides) | 20 min |
| Read 30+ Pages | 40 min |
| Finalize Research (structure your mini-paper) | 45 min |
| Phone-Free Evening (from 7pm onward) | 3+ hrs |

**Journal Prompt:** What big questions are on your mind? What's one thing you've changed your mind about this week?

---

### Day 7: Reclaim Your Mind
**Theme:** Completion, celebration, and planning continuation.

**Quote:** *"Your brain is still yours. Unrot it. Reclaim it. Wake it back up."*

| Task | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Complete Your Project (paper, craft, etc.) | 45 min |
| Finish Book or Read 50+ Pages | 1 hr |
| Full Day of Mindful Phone Use | All day |
| Celebrate & Reflect (comprehensive journal entry) | 20 min |
| Plan Week 2 (what habits continue?) | 15 min |

**Journal Prompt:** How do you feel compared to 7 days ago? What version of yourself showed up this week?

## 5. Technical Specifications

### 5.1 Technology Stack

| Component | Technology |
|-----------|------------|
| Frontend | Vanilla HTML, CSS, JavaScript (no framework dependencies) |
| Storage | Browser localStorage for persistence |
| Hosting | GitHub Pages (static hosting) |
| PWA Support | Web App Manifest for installability |
| Offline | Self-contained single HTML file |

### 5.2 Data Model

All data is stored locally in the browser's localStorage under the key `brainRehabState`:

```javascript
{
  currentDay: 1,                    // Number (1-7) tracking active day
  completedTasks: {                 // Object mapping task IDs to completion status
    "d1t1": true,
    "d1t2": false,
    // ...
  },
  journals: {                       // Object mapping day numbers to journal entry text
    1: "Today I noticed...",
    2: "The boredom was...",
    // ...
  },
  stats: {                          // Tracking metrics
    phoneFreeHours: 12,
    boredMinutes: 45,
    wordsWritten: 1250,
    pagesRead: 87
  },
  startDate: "2026-01-19T00:00:00Z" // ISO timestamp of program start
}
```

## 6. User Flows

### 6.1 First-Time User

```
1. User opens app for the first time
2. App initializes with Day 1 selected, all tasks unchecked
3. User sees motivational quote and daily tasks
4. User completes tasks throughout the day, checking them off
5. User writes journal entry in evening
6. Progress updates in real-time; data persists across sessions
```

### 6.2 Returning User

```
1. User opens app from home screen
2. Previous progress is loaded from localStorage
3. User sees their streak and continues where they left off
4. User can review past journal entries and overall progress
```

### 6.3 Installing as PWA (iOS)

```
1. User opens URL in Safari
2. User taps Share button
3. User selects "Add to Home Screen"
4. App appears as icon on home screen with standalone experience
```

### 6.4 Installing as PWA (Android)

```
1. User opens URL in Chrome
2. User taps menu (⋮)
3. User selects "Add to Home Screen"
4. App appears as icon with full-screen experience
```

## Appendix B: Source Article Summary

### Title: "Unrot Your Brain"

### Core Narrative
The author describes their journey from being a voracious reader and "the smart girl" to experiencing cognitive atrophy post-graduation. The constant scroll, skim, and distraction has replaced deep thinking.

### Key Themes

1. **Identity tied to intelligence:** Being "smart" was a core identity, and losing mental sharpness feels like losing oneself.

2. **The slide from rest to rot:** After burnout, rest is needed, but without intention, rest becomes decay.

3. **Rehabilitation, not optimization:** The goal isn't productivity—it's feeling like yourself again.

4. **Practical exercises:** The article provides specific, actionable strategies:
   - Phone fasting (3+ days to reset)
   - Intentional boredom (10-15 min daily)
   - Freewriting to hear your own thoughts
   - Fascination projects (self-assigned "homework")
   - Physical books and reading challenges
   - Hands-on hobbies requiring focus

### Emotional Core
> "Your brain isn't broken. You haven't peaked. It's still in there—that sharp, curious, wide-eyed version of you."
